legal principles   after the breakdown of a de facto relationship, a court may make such orders as it considers proper for the maintenance of one of the parties to the de facto relationship.   in exercising jurisdiction under section 90se (after being satisfied of the matters in subsections 44(5) and (6) and sections 90sb and 90sd), the court must apply the principle that a party to a de facto relationship must maintain the other party to the de facto relationship: (a) only to the extent that the first-mentioned party is reasonably able to do so, and (b) only if the second-mentioned party is unable to support him or herself adequately whether: (i) by reason of having the care and control of a child of the de facto relationship who has not attained the age of 18 years, or (ii) a
